Current teaching point

Base Shape: 2-3-1

Two defenders stay connected, three midfielders give width and a middle option, and one striker stays high to stretch the field.

Coach cue: “Make a big triangle around the ball: one behind, one beside, one ahead.”

Best play right now

Keep the team shape.

  • Spread out when your team has the ball.
  • Move closer to support when the ball is near you.
  • Stay balanced so we do not all chase the ball.

Option tree for the player on the ball

#1 GK currently has the ball. Choose a soccer action and watch the shape react.

Teaching note: Build simple triangles and choose the next pass based on support behind, beside, and ahead.

How to use it

  • Move Ball: drag the ball or tap a player or area. The ball will snap onto the closest player.
  • Pass Between Players: tap a teammate and the ball will pass to them.
  • Yellow ring: shows who currently has the ball.
  • Show Defense: turns on a reactive defense so players can read pressure, cover, and balance.
  • Ask players: “Where is pressure? Can we play away from it, around it, or through it?”

Grid teaching points

  • Left lane / right lane: wide mids stretch the field and create room inside.
  • Center lane: CM and striker combine to play through the middle.
  • Buildout third: use simple triangles with keeper and backs.
  • Middle third: look for give-and-go, overlap, or switch.
  • Attacking third: cross, cut inside, or play deep into the corner.

Player jobs in a 2-3-1

#1 GK: Pass to a defender or wide mid. Be brave but simple.
#2 / #3 Defenders: Get wide, open body, protect the middle if we lose it.
#4 / #6 Wide mids: Stay wide, dribble forward, combine, or overlap.
#5 CM: Be the helper. Turn if open. Switch fields when crowded.
#7 ST: Stay high, check back, turn to goal, or run into the corner.
Everyone: After passing, move to a new window.

Coach activity

Freeze game: move the ball, pause, and ask: “What lane is the ball in? What third is the ball in? Who supports behind? Who supports wide? Who can run forward?” Then let players copy it on the field.
